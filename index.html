Work

My journey as a developer started at Amazon.com in 2004. As part of the inventory control team, I built tools to reduce inventory defects. These tools made defect detection 4 times faster, and over time reduced defects rates from 5 per million to 3 per million. This resulted in $300,000 in employee bonuses in 2007 alone. I was recognized by the HR department for my work and was commisioned to incorporate my knowledge of employee quality into a larger system to track safety, quality, productivity, and attendance. It was a great 6 years Amazon. I was given the space to learn Amazon's technologies and the freedom to create value for the company where I could.

Later, I worked as the IT manager of a residential retreat centre where I built a database that streamlined registration and finance processes, helping to improve operational performance.

Athough I had learned a lot about being a developer at Amazon, I wanted to return to school to fill in some gaps in my knowledge. I was attracted bo the unusual pairing of computer science and sustainability offered at Dalhousie University. At Dalhousie, I learned to use technology to work with problems related to climate change, economics, and human communication; explored cutting edge technologies like elasticsearch and d3.js; and applied my newfound skills to several interesting projects for employers like Springtide Collective, the Ecology Action Centre, and the Dalhousie Social Media Lab.
